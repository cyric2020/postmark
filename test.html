Key Concepts

Passive Reference Configuration

Definition: The passive reference configuration is a fundamental concept where current enters the positive polarity of the voltage. This ensures that energy flow directionality aligns correctly for power calculations. Slide Reference: Slide 5 Example: For a current source I=5A I = 5 \text{A} I=5A in series with a 10V source, P=V⋅I=50W P = V \cdot I = 50 \text{W} P=V⋅I=50W.

Equations

P=V⋅I P = V \cdot I P=V⋅I (Slide 5) Positive power indicates energy absorption by the element; negative power implies it supplies energy.

Case Studies

Configuration A (Slide 6): Circuit: Current source of 5A, voltage source of 10V. Power Calculation: P=V⋅I=10V×5A=50W P = V \cdot I = 10 \text{V} \times 5\text{A} = 50 \text{W} P=V⋅I=10V×5A=50W.
Configuration B (Slide 6): Circuit: Current source of -5A, voltage source of 10V. Power Calculation: P=V⋅I=10V×(−5A)=−50W P = V \cdot I = 10 \text{V} \times (-5\text{A}) = -50 \text{W} P=V⋅I=10V×(−5A)=−50W.
Configuration C (Slide 6): Circuit: Current source of 5A, voltage source of -10V. Power Calculation: P=V⋅I=(−10V)×5A=−50W P = V \cdot I = (-10 \text{V}) \times 5\text{A} = -50 \text{W} P=V⋅I=(−10V)×5A=−50W.
